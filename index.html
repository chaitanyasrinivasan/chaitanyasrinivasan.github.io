Research

I am broadly interested in designing computational models to study the genomic basis of neurological disorders. Currently, I am studying brain region and cell type specific epigenomics of addiction.

Addiction-associated genetic variants implicate cell type- and region-specific cis-regulatory elements in addiction neurobiology (in progress)
Chaitanya Srinivasan*, BaDoi N. Phan*, Alyssa J. Lawler, Easwaran Ramamurthy, Michael Kleyman, Ashley R. Brown, Irene M. Kaplow, Morgan E. Wirthlin, Andreas R. Pfenning
NIDA Genetic Consortium Abstract

We present a framework that maps addiction-associated genetic variation to brain region- and cell type-specific putative cis-regulatory enhancers across human and mouse epigenomic profiles. We further attribute cell-type specific heritability of addiction genetic risk using machine learning models.

Using metabolomics and lipidomics to crack the code on asparaginase-associated pancreatitis (AAP)
Chaitanya Srinivasan*, Li Wen, Amitava Mukherjee, Sohail Z. Husain
Carnegie Mellon University, Meeting of the Minds Symposium, 2018.
Won Osher Lifelong Learning Institute Award
American Pancreatic Association Abstract

We used a non-targeted metabolomic and lipidomic platform to determine whether there are unique biomarker signatures that predict the development of asparaginase-associated pancreatitis.
